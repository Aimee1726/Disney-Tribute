Walt Disney

1901 - 1966

Life of a Legend

  • Born December 5, 1901 Chicago, Il
  • Disney discovers an interest in drawing at a young age, when he was paid to draw a horse for a neighbor. He begins to practice his skill by copying the cartoons from the paper.
  • Being too young to enlist in the army, Disney forged the date on his birth certificate and joined the Red Cross as an ambulance driver. He was sent to France but arrived in November after the armistice.
  • Disney gets his first job as an apprentice artist in 1919. This is where he develops his craft as cartoon artist.
  • In 1923, Disney moves to Hollywood to pursue a career as a live-action film director.
  • Through a series of failures and success, Disney goes on to become one of the greatest animators of all time. He becomes a trailblazer in the animation industry, using advanced technologies to perfect his craft.
  • Died December 15, 1966 Burbank, Ca

Achievements and Contributions

  • Creates the first cartoon with synchronized sound in 1927 with Steamboat Willie
  • Develops a multiplane camera while creating the first full-length feature animation, Snow White in 1937. The camera adds depth and realism to the animation.
  • Disney continues to make his mark with more animation movies as well as nature documenteries, television shows and surprisingly, training films for the military to help with the war efforts.
  • Disney dreams of opening a theme park, where families can enjoy themselves in a clean and beautiful setting. In 1955 Disneyland opens.
  • Walt Disney has won 22 Academy Awards, 1 Emmy and The Cecil B. DeMille Award.
